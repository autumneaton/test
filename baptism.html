What We Learned about Baptism and Family

What Autumn Learned:

I learned that baptism is the first step in being part of a covenant family. Baptisms in the temple are extra special to families because it is a proxy ordinance that everyone can do together.


What Conrad Learned:

I learned the importance of helping other families be a part of God's covenantal family. When we do baptisms in the temple for others, I am reminded of how blessed I am to be baptized while on earth with my family. Being Baptized while on the earth richens the family experience. It sets sacred and righteous expectations for how each member of the family will live and treat each other.

What is Baptism?

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints teaches that baptism is a sacred ordinance that is necessary for salvation. It brings us closer to God through Jesus Christ.

What are Baptisms for the Dead?

Baptisms for the dead are performed by proxy in temples. This means that living Church members are baptized on behalf of deceased individuals.
